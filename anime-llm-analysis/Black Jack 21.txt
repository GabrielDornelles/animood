"Black Jack 21" plunges viewers into a labyrinth of moral ambiguity and existential inquiry, as the enigmatic surgeon Black Jack grapples with the shadowy ramifications of his past. The narrative is steeped in a somber atmosphere, echoing themes of loss, betrayal, and the relentless quest for truth. As Black Jack delves into the conspiracy surrounding the tragic bombing that claimed his mother's life, the series explores profound psychological and philosophical dilemmas akin to those found in works like "Monster" and "Paranoia Agent." 

The pacing is deliberate, allowing for deep character introspection and a gradual unraveling of the dark secrets tied to an organization and Project "Noir," all while maintaining a tone that oscillates between gravity and moments of levity through quirky supporting characters, including a police lieutenant providing comic relief. The juxtaposition of humor against a backdrop of tragedy heightens the emotional stakes, resonating with viewers who appreciate the complexity of human experience.

The richly detailed art style and nuanced storytelling are testaments to Osamu Tezuka's legacy, offering a near-masterpiece experience that invites contemplation on the ethics of medicine and the weight of personal history.
