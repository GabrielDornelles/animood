This OVA serves as a reflective interlude that dives deep into the past of Yuuki's parents, particularly through the eyes of his mother, Midori. The narrative unfolds with a delicate balance of nostalgia and melancholy, as it reveals the struggles and sacrifices that shape their identities. The atmosphere is steeped in a somber yet intimate tone, inviting viewers to confront themes of love, loss, and the burdens of heritage.

The psychological depth of the characters resonates with fans who appreciate narratives that challenge the moral implications of their existence as vampires, reflecting on what it means to be human in a world filled with darkness.

This anime appeals to viewers seeking a contemplative experience, particularly those who find solace in stories that delve into the complexities of family dynamics and personal history.
