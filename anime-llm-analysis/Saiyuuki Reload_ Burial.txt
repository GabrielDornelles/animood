"Saiyuuki Reload: Burial" delves into the intricate backstories of beloved characters, weaving a rich tapestry of past struggles and emotional growth. Set in a world steeped in myth and existential questions, this OVA series explores themes of redemption, friendship, and the haunting weight of one's past. The narrative unfolds with a contemplative pace, allowing viewers to absorb the depths of each character's psyche. 

The story invites comparisons to other anime like "Baccano!" and "Fate/Zero," as it intricately balances moments of intense action with profound philosophical reflections on fate and choice. Sanzo’s early relationship with his master, juxtaposed with Goku's first encounter with him, offers a poignant exploration of mentorship and the burdens of legacy. Hakkai’s internal conflict following his dark past and the solace he finds in Gojyo's companionship highlights the emotional resonance of seeking forgiveness and understanding.

The atmosphere is a blend of somber introspection and fleeting moments of levity, creating a complex emotional landscape that resonates deeply with viewers who appreciate character-driven narratives. The art style enhances this mood, employing a gritty yet beautiful aesthetic that mirrors the turmoil within the characters. 

This anime particularly appeals to those in search of a reflective viewing experience, inviting audiences to grapple with their own emotions while navigating the trials of the Saiyuki quartet. It is perfect for viewers who enjoy stories that challenge the mind and tug at the heart, making it an ideal choice for a somber or contemplative mood.

Mood tags: introspective, emotional, philosophical, character-driven, dark themes, redemption
