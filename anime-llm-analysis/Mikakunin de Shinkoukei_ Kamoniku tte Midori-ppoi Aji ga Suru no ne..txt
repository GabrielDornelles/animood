In this charming OVA, the intertwining stories of Mashiro and Kobeni evoke nostalgia and warmth, drawing viewers into a reflective exploration of childhood memories and the bonds that shape us. As Kobeni recounts her playful days with friends Benio and Nadeshiko, the narrative unfolds with a gentle, whimsical tone, reminiscent of slice-of-life gems like "March Comes in Like a Lion" and "Barakamon." Mashiro's recollections of her serene mountain life further deepen the emotional landscape, contrasting the simplicity of rural existence with the complexities of their current lives.

The atmosphere is infused with a sense of wonder and a hint of melancholy, as both characters navigate the elusive nature of memory and identity. Themes of friendship, self-discovery, and the bittersweet passage of time resonate throughout, appealing to those who appreciate character-driven narratives that delve into the human experience. The pacing is leisurely, allowing viewers to savor each moment and reflection, creating a cozy ambiance that invites introspection.

This OVA particularly resonates with viewers seeking comfort, nostalgia, or a gentle reminder of the importance of connections in their lives. It caters to those in a contemplative mood, looking for a heartwarming escape that balances lighthearted storytelling with deeper philosophical undertones about the essence of memory and belonging.
