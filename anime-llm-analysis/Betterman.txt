In the shadowy depths of a post-apocalyptic landscape, "Betterman" immerses viewers in a haunting narrative that blends psychological horror with existential dread. The story revolves around Keita Aono, an unassuming high school student whose life spirals into chaos after he unwittingly stumbles into Bottom World, a derelict amusement park steeped in mystery and violence. This descent into the surreal mirrors the themes of isolation and fear seen in classics like "Neon Genesis Evangelion" and "Serial Experiments Lain," as it explores the impact of technology on human existence.

As Keita teams up with his childhood friend Hinoki Sai, the atmosphere thickens with tension and suspense. The duo pilots a formidable robot against malevolent attractions that have taken on a life of their own, reflecting the struggle against an overwhelming and incomprehensible force, dubbed "Algernon." This allegorical battle against twisted technology invites viewers to ponder the fragility of the human psyche and the moral implications of innovation. The series evokes a palpable sense of unease, enhanced by its eerie visual style and pacing that oscillates between moments of frenetic action and introspective quietude.
