In the hauntingly beautiful world of *Planetarian: Snow Globe*, viewers are immersed in a post-apocalyptic landscape where hope flickers like a distant star. This emotional prequel delves into the past of Yumemi, a cheerful robot, whose unwavering optimism stands in stark contrast to the desolation surrounding her. The narrative unfolds with a gentle pace, allowing the audience to savor moments of warmth and nostalgia, reminiscent of the themes explored in *Anohana* and *Your Lie in April*. As Yumemi navigates her existence within the remnants of a forgotten planetarium, the story beautifully intertwines psychological depth with philosophical musings on memory and the essence of humanity.

This OVA resonates with viewers seeking a reflective experience, one that stirs feelings of longing and introspection. The rich atmospheric elements, underscored by a poignant soundtrack, evoke a sense of melancholic beauty, drawing comparisons to *Violet Evergarden* and *The Garden of Words*. Fans of character-driven narratives will appreciate the emotional weight carried by Yumemi's unwavering spirit, as well as the subtle exploration of companionship amidst desolation.

While some may find the short length a drawback, the emotional impact is undeniable, crafting a tapestry of feelings that lingers long after viewing. Those in search of a poignant, heartwarming tale that balances sorrow with joy will find solace in *Planetarian: Snow Globe*, making it a perfect choice for quiet, contemplative evenings or moments of reflection.
